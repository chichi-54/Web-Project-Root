Career & College Readiness Resources

Explore tools, guides, and programs designed to support your journey from high school into college or the workforce. Whether you’re writing a resume, exploring career paths, or researching colleges, this section gives you guidance and practical next steps.

Career Exploration & Planning

Figuring out your interests and how they connect to future careers is one of the first steps toward success after high school. Many programs help students explore their strengths and match them with potential careers.

  • Student Career Pathway Tools – BigFuture: A free online resource where students can research careers, explore options like apprenticeships, college programs, and vocational paths, and match interests with future opportunities. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}
  • Scoir Career Readiness Tools: Offers assessments to help discover interests and match them with career clusters and college programs. Includes detailed career profiles and recommended pathways. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}
  • CalCRN (California Career Resource Network): Career guides and planning resources that help students discover job and training paths. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Resume Writing & Job Search Tools

Building a strong resume and knowing how to find job openings — even without much experience — is a key skill. Below are some tools and guides that can help you get started.

  • Jobs & Opportunities for Teens: Many districts list teen‑friendly jobs and internship options, along with tips on building a resume, interview prep, and networking skills. For example, school career centers often offer resume templates and document checklists. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
  • WayUp (Job Matching Platform): A job and internship platform that connects students and recent grads with employers based on skills and interests. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
  • Career Planning Workshops: Many colleges and career centers provide workshops on resume writing, interviewing techniques, and building job‑search skills. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

College Readiness Resources

Preparing for college means researching schools, understanding entrance requirements, exploring majors, and planning for financial aid. These tools guide you through that process step by step.

  • College Planning Tools: Many high schools and counselors use programs like Naviance to help students prepare for college by tracking tests, comparing admission data, and managing application tasks. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
  • Career Readiness Curriculum Modules: Programs like School‑Connect provide structured modules for setting goals, identifying strengths, and understanding higher education options including financial aid and college applications. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}
  • National PTA College & Career Resources: Guides on choosing between trade school, college, or apprenticeships, and free assessments to match your interests with career fields. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

Additional Supports & Real‑World Experience

Getting hands‑on experience — through internships, mock interviews, or volunteer work — can boost your confidence and help you stand out to employers or college admissions teams.

  • Many school districts partner with local businesses and community programs to offer job‑readiness workshops, mock interviews, and career fairs where students can interact directly with employers. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}
  • Look for community programs (4‑H, local workforce commissions, youth employment days) that provide career exploration activities, resume reviews, and interview practice. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}
